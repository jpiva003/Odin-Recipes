Empanadas (South America)

Description

Empanadas are a popular pastry in many South American countries, with variations in fillings and preparation methods. They consist of dough stuffed with a variety of ingredients such as beef, chicken, cheese, or vegetables, then baked or fried. Empanadas are enjoyed as a snack or a meal and reflect the diverse culinary influences found across the continent. Each country has its unique twist on the empanada, making it a versatile and beloved dish throughout South America.

Some ingredients

  • Dough (can be made from flour, water, salt, and butter or lard)
  • Fillings (such as ground beef, chicken, cheese, or vegetables)
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Spices (such as cumin, paprika, and oregano)
  • Hard-boiled eggs (optional, common in some variations)
  • Olives or raisins (optional, depending on regional variations)

    • Steps to make Empanadas

    1. Make Dough: Mix flour, salt, butter (or lard), and water. Let rest.
    2. Prepare Filling: Cook ground meat with onions, garlic, and spices.
    3. Assemble: Fill dough circles with meat, fold, and seal edges.
    4. Cook: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes or fry until golden.
